EMRStdAgent: An Intelligent Framework Based on Large Language Models for Solving Medical Data Standardization and Cross-Language Terminology Matching Challenges

Lijuan Wu1,2#, Jinxin Huang1#, Hongnian Wang5#, Liyi Mai1, Yuxin Fan1, Xueyun Zhan1, Xinrong He1, Abdelouahab Bellou1,3,4*

Abstract

The standardization of laboratory data is essential for achieving interoperability in multi-center electronic medical records (EMRs), yet cross-linguistic terminology mapping remains a significant challenge. Existing approaches, often based on rule-based or simple similarity-matching methods, struggle with language variations and semantic ambiguities, limiting their effectiveness. To address this, we propose EMRStdAgent, an intelligent framework that integrates multi-stage vector retrieval, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)-based reasoning, and expert validation to enhance LOINC code mapping. Our experiments on the MIMIC and GDPH datasets show that EMRStdAgent achieves approximately 90% accuracy, significantly outperforming traditional methods. This research provides a scalable and efficient solution for medical data standardization, paving the way for improved global healthcare data interoperability.
